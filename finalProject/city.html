History

Idaho Falls was originally settled by ranchers, but in 1864 the settlement began to grow because of Harry Rickets business mentality. He created a ferry srevice that crossed the Snake river. By 1865 the settlement had the makings of a small town and were known as Taylor's Crossing and then became known as Eagle Rock by 1866. This location helped westward migration on the Montana Trail. Eventually a railway was built through the narrowing of the river at Taylor's Crossing. The town really started to grow when the railway came through because of a surge of homesteading the local area. The railroad facilities were mostly moved to Pocatello in 1887 due to a worker's strike and Eagle Rock. The name was changed to Idaho Falls instead of Eagle Rock to help calm the minds of potential farmers coming to the area.
